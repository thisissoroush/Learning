# Chapter 7: Stay Aligned with Authority
## *Your Power Is Loaned — Learn to Keep It*

---

## 🎯 Core Concept

Authority in Staff+ roles isn't inherent — it's **borrowed** from a more senior leader. What's bestowed can be retracted. Remaining effective long-term requires mastering the art of staying aligned with the organizational authority that enables your work.

> *"I serve at the pleasure of the Vice President."* — Rick Boone

## 🛡️ Beyond the Safety Net

Up until Staff, your company's systems support you — promotion pipelines, manager coaching, structured feedback. At Staff+, that safety net largely disappears.

```
JUNIOR/MID CAREER:           STAFF+ CAREER:
────────────────────         ──────────────────────────────────
Company sets you up          YOU set the company up for success
Manager guides development   You guide your own development
Safety net catches mistakes  Mistakes have larger blast radius
Path is well-defined         Path requires active navigation
```

> You are now one of the people responsible for setting up the company, your team, and your manager for success — not the other way around.

---

## 🤝 How to Stay Aligned

### 1. Never Surprise Your Manager

Nothing destroys trust faster than surprises. Large organizations require leaders to track many parallel threads. Surprises break rhythm and suggest someone isn't taking responsibility for their domain.

> **Rule:** Treat every time you surprise your manager as an incident to learn from and prevent repeating.

### 2. Don't Let Your Sponsor Surprise You

Managers try to relay relevant information but often fail. Don't wait for it — go get it:
- Send **weekly email updates** or Slack summaries of your focuses
- In 1:1s, explicitly ask: "Are there areas I should be more focused on?"
- Ask: "How do my current priorities align with yours?"

### 3. Feed Your Manager's Context

Your manager shouldn't learn about team frustrations or scaling failures from someone else first. Proactively surface:
- Team morale issues
- Process friction
- Technology that's not scaling
- Stakeholder concerns

> Frame it clearly: "I'm not bringing this as a problem for you to solve — just context I think is useful to have."

## 🧠 The Rick Boone Model

Rick Boone, as Strategic Advisor to Uber's VP of Infrastructure, describes his approach:

> *"I go into a room and think: 'What would Matthew do here? What is the question he would want to ask?' Because I can't always run back to him for clarification, it's essential to develop and maintain a deep understanding of his world view."*

This requires:
- Deep knowledge of your sponsor's values, priorities, and reasoning patterns
- Willingness to stake your reputation on extending their vision
- Belief in the direction yourself — otherwise advocacy becomes disingenuous

## ✅ Key Takeaways

| # | Takeaway |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Staff authority is borrowed** — it can be retracted; stay aligned to keep it |
| 2 | **The safety net disappears** at Staff+; you're responsible for creating your own conditions for success |
