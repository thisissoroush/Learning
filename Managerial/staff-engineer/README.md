## 🎯 What This Book Is About

Most engineers reach **Senior Engineer** in 5–8 years and then face a fork in the road: management track or technical leadership track? Books exist in abundance for the management path. Almost nothing exists for the other path — until this book.

**Staff Engineer** is the definitive guide to reaching and thriving in Staff-plus (Staff, Principal, Distinguished) individual contributor roles. It answers the questions that keep ambitious engineers up at night:
- What do Staff engineers *actually* do all day?
- Is the title worth pursuing, and will I even enjoy the work?
- How do I get promoted without a formal process?
- When should I switch companies to get the title?

> **The central insight:** A Staff Engineer is not a "better Senior Engineer." It is a fundamentally *different job* — one that requires new skills, new behaviors, and a new relationship with organizational authority.

## 🔑 Key Terms Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Staff-plus** | Umbrella term for Staff, Principal, Distinguished Engineer roles |
| **Career level** | The level (typically Senior Eng) where companies stop expecting promotion |
| **Tech Lead** | Archetype: guides team approach and execution; most common Staff type |
| **Architect** | Archetype: owns a critical technical domain across the org |
| **Solver** | Archetype: digs deep into critical problems, moves on when solved |
| **Right Hand** | Archetype: extends senior executive's reach with borrowed authority |
| **Snacking** | Doing easy, low-impact work that feels productive but isn't |
| **Preening** | Doing low-impact but high-visibility work to look good |
| **Chasing ghosts** | Pursuing large misguided initiatives from previous experience |
| **Glue work** | Necessary but invisible coordination work that keeps teams moving |
| **Sponsorship** | Actively advocating for and advancing another person's career |
| **Promotion packet** | Document detailing your impact, projects, and gaps for promotion |
| **Staff project** | A complex, high-stakes project used to demonstrate Staff-level impact |
| **SCQA** | Situation → Complication → Question → Answer — executive communication format |
| **Nemawashi** | Japanese practice of pre-aligning stakeholders before a formal decision |
| **Meritocrat** | Company that makes exception-heavy, individual-driven decisions |
| **Proceduralist** | Company that prefers consistency-driven, policy-heavy decisions |
