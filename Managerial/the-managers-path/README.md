# 📘 The Manager's Path
### *A Guide for Tech Leaders Navigating Growth and Change*
**Author:** Camille Fournier | **Published:** 2017 | **Publisher:** O'Reilly Media

---

## 🧭 Why This Book Exists

Camille Fournier joined Rent the Runway as a manager with no team, and grew into the CTO role over four years — scaling from zero to full engineering leadership with no safety net and a brutal learning curve. This book is everything she learned, written so you don't have to figure it all out the hard way.

> *"This book is for engineering managers. It's not a generic management book."*

## 🔑 The 5 Big Ideas of This Book

1. **Every level requires a totally different skill set** — what made you great at the previous level doesn't automatically help at the next one.
2. **Technical credibility matters throughout your entire career** — don't abandon your engineering instincts as you rise.
3. **Management is a people-AND-technical discipline** — it's not just HR, it's also architecture, process, and systems thinking.
4. **1-1s are non-negotiable** — they are the single most important tool a manager has.
5. **You learn by doing, not by waiting** — discomfort is the signal that you're growing.
